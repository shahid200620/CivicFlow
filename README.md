# CivicFlow

### Digital Platform for Government-to-Person (G2P) Benefit Delivery

*A modular digital platform designed to support secure, transparent, and efficient delivery of public welfare programs through interoperable digital infrastructure.*

---

## Executive Summary

CivicFlow is a documentation repository based on the concepts and architecture of **OpenG2P**, an open-source Digital Public Good housed at IIIT Bangalore and developed with contributions from an international community. The platform enables governments and humanitarian organizations to digitize the complete Government-to-Person (G2P) benefit delivery lifecycle through modular, interoperable components.

This repository summarizes the platform's architecture, workflows, and implementation concepts for educational and portfolio purposes.

---

## Project Vision（プロジェクトの目的）

Modern public welfare systems require secure, scalable, and transparent digital infrastructure capable of delivering benefits efficiently while reducing administrative complexity.

The proposed platform supports governments in managing beneficiary registration, program administration, digital payments, and operational reporting through a unified digital workflow.

## Problem Statement

Many public welfare programs continue to depend on fragmented systems and manual processes, resulting in:

* Duplicate beneficiary records
* Administrative delays
* Limited interoperability
* Inefficient reporting
* Higher operational costs
* Difficult audit processes

A unified digital platform helps standardize these workflows while improving service delivery and governance.

---

## Solution Overview（ソリューション概要）

The platform adopts a modular architecture that allows governments to implement only the components required for their existing digital ecosystem.

Core capabilities include:

* Digital beneficiary registration
* Program management
* Identity mapping
* Benefit disbursement
* Reporting and analytics
* Administrative dashboards

Its interoperable design enables integration with existing Digital Public Infrastructure rather than replacing operational systems.
